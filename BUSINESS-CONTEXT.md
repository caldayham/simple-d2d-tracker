# cf.design — Business Context

## The Business

Small local carpentry and handyman business run by two brothers in Palo Alto, CA. Website: https://cf.design/

## Proven Track Record

- 50+ completed jobs with zero unsatisfied clients
- Positive replacement rate: every job led to 1-2 new qualified clients/jobs
- Demonstrated service-market fit through organic Nextdoor traction
- 6 official Google reviews, all 5 stars
- Verified local service business on Google
- Strong client relationships (some became friends)

## Original Growth System

1. Complete quality work for a client
2. Post on Nextdoor with clear, interesting images and description
3. Link a Google Form for project submissions + phone number
4. Previous client drops a positive comment on the post
5. Organic traction generates 1-2 new qualified leads per job
6. Positive replacement rate allowed gradual price increases

## Current Challenge

After a break pursuing other endeavors, the original Nextdoor strategy has lost effectiveness. Possible reasons:

- Business appears more "professional" now — posts feel like ads rather than authentic neighbor-helping-neighbor content
- Nextdoor algorithm changes may suppress posts that drive traffic off-platform
- Increased competition from other local handymen on the platform

## What's Been Tried (Post-Return)

- 4 Nextdoor job posts with full strategy — significantly lower engagement
- Re-contacting previous happy clients — moderate success
- Nextdoor ads — 1 quality lead/consult
- Referrals — 1 quality lead
- Craigslist ads
- High-quality printed brochures
- Professional website with intake form
- Instagram and Nextdoor business pages

## Strategic Analysis: Points of Leverage

**Personal strengths:**
- Presentable: young, healthy, articulate, builds trust quickly
- Local: actually lives in the area
- Proven: delivers quality service that benefits both client and business

**Competitive landscape:**
- Nextdoor = competing with every local handyman for attention
- Ads = competing with anyone with a bank account
- Door-to-door = leverages personal strengths, costs only time, predictable output

## Chosen Strategy: Door-to-Door Canvassing

Not the predatory high-pressure sales approach (pest control, solar). Instead: genuine, value-adding conversations with neighbors about carpentry/handyman needs.

**Why door-to-door:**
- Costs nothing but time
- Predictable consultation/job output
- Leverages strongest assets (in-person trust-building, local presence)
- No platform dependency or algorithm risk
- Direct feedback loop for refining approach

**Future consideration:** Every Door Direct Mail (EDDM) as a complementary strategy once door-to-door process is refined.
